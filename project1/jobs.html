    • NAXG2
    • Salary: $85,00 - $105,000 AUD (source)
    • Years of experience: MINIMUM 2 years

    • Brief Description

      • We're looking for a proactive and detail-oriented Network Administrator to join our IT team. In this role, you'll be responsible for managing and maintaining our network infrastructure, ensuring the security, performance, and reliability of systems that support our day-to-day operations.

    • Key Network Administrator Skills

      • Knowledge of different types of networks: Network types include Local Area Networks (LANs), Wide Area Networks (WANs), Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), and sometimes Storage Area Networks (SANs). Though some jobs may require more experience in one over another, having a basic understanding of the various network types and how to configure them will be crucial to your network administrator skill set.
      • Understanding of network security components: These include firewalls, VPNs, and access control. Knowing about common cyber attacks and the appropriate response to them is critical for a network administrator.
      • Familiarity with servers: Upgrading and configuring servers is a sought-after skill for network administrators. These may include Windows and Linux operating systems.
      • Communication and teamwork: As a network administrator, you'll likely work on a team or with other organization members to ensure computers and systems are running smoothly. Being able to communicate problems and solutions with other people is a valuable skill to have.

    • Essential Qualifications and Skills

      • Degree in Information Technology, Computer Science, or related field
      • 1-3 years of experience in a network administration or support role
      • Strong understanding of TCP/IP, LAN/WAN, DNS, DHCP, and VPN
      • Experience with network hardware (e.g., Cisco, HP, Ubiquiti)
      • Familiarity with Windows Server and/or Linux environments
      • Certifications: CompTIA Network+ or CCNA
      • Strong analytical and communication skills
      • Ability to work independently and prioritize tasks effectively



  2. Systems Engineer

    • SE7M5
    • Salary: $100,000 - $130,000 AUD (source)
    • Years of experience: MINIMUM 3 years

    • Brief Description

      • A systems engineer is a professional who applies engineering principles to develop and maintain efficient systems. They play a crucial role in various industries by solving problems in systems. Unlike other engineers who solve issues in independent parts, a systems engineer takes a holistic approach by studying the interconnections of systems. When a systems engineer develops a system, they ensure it provides a positive user experience while considering the company objectives and operations.

    • Key Systems Engineer Skills and Requirements

      • Problem-Solving and Critical Thinking: Developing innovative solutions to complex challenges by analyzing systems holistically.
      • Effective Communication: Clearly conveying technical information to diverse stakeholders, facilitating collaboration and ensuring project alignment.
      • Time Management and Prioritization: Balancing multiple projects and deadlines efficiently to maintain productivity.
      • Adaptability and Flexibility: Adjusting to evolving technologies and project requirements in a dynamic environment.
      • Systems Architecture Design and Modeling: Creating and analyzing system structures to ensure they meet functional and performance requirements.
      • Programming and Scripting Languages: Proficiency in languages such as Python, Java, or C++ for system development and automation.
      • Network Configuration and Security: Understanding network protocols and implementing security measures to protect system integrity.
      • Cloud Infrastructure and Services: Experience with cloud platforms and services to design scalable and reliable systems.
      • Automation and Orchestration Tools: Utilizing tools to automate system processes and improve efficiency.
      • Continuous Learning: Staying updated with technological advancements to incorporate new solutions and maintain system relevance.
